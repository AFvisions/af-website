Carnation Bridge

Role: Project Manager, UX Contributor, Graphic Designer
Team Size: 4
Tools: Figma (FigJam), PowerPoint
Project Type: UX Design Case Study (Conceptual)

Overview

Carnation Bridge is a funeral planning app designed to guide individuals through emotionally difficult moments with clarity and compassion. We built a resource-rich, decision-simplifying tool to support immediate-need funeral planning, offering guidance, vendor discovery, and emotional support tools in one place.

The Problem

Funeral planning often happens at a time of shock, grief, and uncertainty. Most families aren’t prepared, especially when arrangements haven’t been pre-planned. The result is stress, confusion, and difficulty making informed choices.

The Solution

  • Location- and budget-based funeral home search
  • AI-powered chat guidance
  • Grief counseling resources
  • Step-by-step checklists
  • Price transparency and service comparisons

Research & Discovery

We conducted user interviews with three key user types: those who had never planned a funeral, those who had pre-planned, and those who had done so recently after a death. Interviews were guided by a script designed for empathy and sensitivity.

Persona: Meet Diane

Diane is planning a funeral for her late father with limited funds and no pre-arrangements. She needs help organizing tasks, securing financial support, and honoring her father’s wishes on a tight timeline.

  • Wants: A respectful, non-religious memorial; to follow her father's wishes
  • Needs: Financial assistance; organization tools; funeral within 3 weeks

Journey Mapping

We mapped the emotional and logistical landscape of an unprepared funeral planner and found stress peaks during document collection, vendor research, and cost estimation.

The Breakthrough

Our initial solution was overly broad — trying to handle everything. It wasn’t until we mapped the journey that we realized the most critical opportunity:

⚡ Helping users connect with the right funeral home as early as possible.

That insight allowed us to tighten the scope and build around a core experience: calm, guided, human-first support during urgent funeral planning.

Reflection

Carnation Bridge taught me that clarity comes from empathy and focus. By narrowing the problem space and leading with user stories, we created something both helpful and emotionally respectful. This project sharpened my UX process and reinforced my commitment to user-first design.
